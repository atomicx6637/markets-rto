# Stakeholder Questions for Market Entry Project

This document provides a structured list of questions to ask key stakeholders when planning a project to enter a new energy market (RTO or Markets+). The questions are categorized by the typical role of the stakeholder.

---

### 1. For Executive Leadership & Strategy

*(Goal: Understand the business drivers, budget, risk tolerance, and definition of success.)*

*   **Strategic Goals:** What are the top 3 strategic objectives we aim to achieve by joining this market? (e.g., reduce wholesale energy costs, improve grid reliability, meet decarbonization targets, generate new revenue streams).
*   **Success Metrics:** How will we measure the success of this initiative a year after go-live? What are the specific Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) we should track?
*   **Budget & Resources:** What is the overall budget for the selection, implementation, and first year of operation? Are resources (personnel, capital) allocated for this?
*   **Risk Appetite:** What is our tolerance for financial risk associated with market participation? Are there established limits on potential losses or trading strategies?
*   **Strategic Path:** Are we viewing participation in Markets+ as a final destination, or as a strategic stepping stone towards full RTO membership in the future?
*   **Timeline:** What are the key deadlines or business drivers that dictate our go-live timeline?

---

### 2. For Trading & Portfolio Management

*(Goal: Understand how the team will interact with the market and manage financial positions.)*

*   **Market Products:** Which specific market products do we plan to participate in initially? (e.g., Day-Ahead Energy, Real-Time Energy, Ancillary Services). Which products might we consider in the future (e.g., Capacity Markets, Financial Transmission Rights - FTRs)?
*   **Bidding Strategy:** How complex are our intended bidding strategies? Will we need to model complex generation assets, create virtual bids, or co-optimize multiple assets?
*   **User Interface:** What information is critical for a trader to see on a primary dashboard? What tools are needed for bid entry, position monitoring, and market analysis?
*   **Shadow Settlement:** What are the requirements for our settlement and reconciliation process? How will we validate market statements and manage billing disputes?
*   **Reporting:** What reports are necessary to analyze profit and loss (P&L), assess portfolio risk, and evaluate the performance of our bidding strategies?
*   **Automation:** To what extent should bidding, data analysis, and other routine tasks be automated?

---

### 3. For Operations & Reliability

*(Goal: Understand the impact on the control room and physical asset management.)*

*   **Real-Time Dispatch:** How will our control room operators receive, validate, and respond to real-time dispatch instructions from the market operator?
*   **System Integration:** What is the workflow for communicating dispatch instructions from the new market system to our existing Energy Management System (EMS) or SCADA?
*   **Outage Management:** What is our current process for scheduling and communicating generation and transmission outages? How does this need to adapt to the market's requirements?
*   **Operator Training:** What new skills and training will our control room operators need to effectively participate in the market?
*   **Reliability:** What new procedures are needed to ensure we meet our reliability obligations while operating under market direction?

---

### 4. For IT & Technology

*(Goal: Understand the technical landscape, integration points, and support model.)*

*   **System Landscape:** Can you provide a map of the existing systems that a new market platform would need to integrate with (e.g., EMS, SCADA, ETRM, Data Historian, enterprise financials)?
*   **Build vs. Buy:** What are our criteria for deciding whether to build a solution in-house versus purchasing a vendor product?
*   **Data Architecture:** Where will the high-volume market data (prices, schedules, etc.) be stored? What are our data retention policies? Do we have a preferred time-series database technology?
*   **Technical Specs:** What are the organization's standards for system availability, disaster recovery, security, and data latency?
*   **Cybersecurity:** Beyond NERC CIP, what are our internal cybersecurity policies that this system must adhere to? How will we manage secure access for users and APIs?
*   **Support Model:** Who will be responsible for the 24/7 support of this new system and its connection to the market operator? What is the planned support structure?

---

### 5. For Regulatory, Compliance & Legal

*(Goal: Understand the legal obligations and compliance framework.)*

*   **Compliance Monitoring:** How will we monitor and document our adherence to all market rules and NERC/FERC regulations?
*   **Legal Review:** What is the process for reviewing and approving the legal agreements required for market participation?
*   **Reporting:** What are the mandatory reports we must submit to regulatory bodies? Who is responsible for generating them?
*   **Data Retention:** What are the specific legal and regulatory requirements for retaining market communications, bid data, and settlement records? For how long must they be kept?
*   **Rule Changes:** What is the process for tracking changes to market rules and ensuring our systems and procedures are updated accordingly?
